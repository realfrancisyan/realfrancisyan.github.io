How I Use Two Great iPhone Apps to Improve Creativity

Niice

Niice is a pretty simple tool that helps you quickly search out the photos that would inspire you. With its gorgeous website which enables you to draw inspiration with your computer, Niice has a companion iPhone application available for free on the App Store that keeps you inspired, organizes your best picks, etc. Surprise me, one of Niice’s key features, makes searching a lot interesting, as it is like “I am feeling lucky” from Google that could provide some great bits of images even when you do not come up with an idea of what you really want to create at the moment.

Niice’s iPhone App makes it easy to search photos for inspiration

Fleck

Compared with Niice, Fleck is much more different. Sure enough you could browse images of any kind, but with its clean design and the availability to upload your own images that could be delivered to Fleck community, it is more like designers’ Instagram. The only drawback that I find is the app’s lack of search engine (or I might miss it). With limited categories shown in the app, I could merely discover some of the themes that I am fond of, such as Graphic Design, Architecture, Illustration and more. However, the quality of these photographs is much higher, as a whole.

Fleck App Screen Shots

Conclusion

For many designers, gathering inspiration is never an easy task to cope with. Every day I utilize these two apps to inspire me to create better web pages. But as design always comes with time and patience, I still need to make great efforts on learning and crafting. In the end, I wish you all could somewhat benefit from what I have written and continue to step forward.
