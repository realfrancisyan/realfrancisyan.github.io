My Ultimate Design Inspirations 2015

For years, I have been looking for some kind of tutorials, websites and resources which can actually help me improve my design. This requires time, efforts, tactics and most importantly, patience. For many designers, they could probably throw their works that have been done for countless days and nights away as a result of the dissatisfaction of their design. Nobody could always be creative until he finds something that is inspirational. The following list of web pages may help you get hold of the latest trend of design, and some resources are provided, especially for web designers.

GURA FIKU

Conducted by designer Ryan Hageman, GURA FIKU is a collection of visual research surveying the history of graphic design in Japan. As an accumulation of Japanese posters possibly produced by students and organizations in Japan, this collection could offer proper guidance to you to get inspirations, enjoy the strong visual experience that you encounter, and ultimately, start to think about your own works.

SiteInspire

In case you miss it, SiteInspire is a gorgeous-looking websites collection curated by professionals, with frequent updates each day. Attention web designers, it is highly recommended to check it out at least once a week to get inspired.

Alternatives:

Colors

As designers, we all love colors, right? But matching the correct colors is not always easy. Fortunately, some tools could do this for us.

Palettab

Palettab is a chrome extension that could automatically pick the right colors for you. Besides, it could indicate which font suits the color you are currently picking best. You can also intuitively copy the color hex code to your notes app, photoshop, anywhere. It is one of my favorite color pickers to improve my overall productivity.

Alternatives:

  • Coolors
  • NIPPON COLORS
  • RGB•5

Resources

oozled

Introduced on Medium, oozled is a site that curates resources of any kind for designers to do web projects. If that interests you, take a look and see what might suit you.

Alternatives:

  • Freebiesbug
  • Pixeden

Photos

When our audiences browse a web page, what would be the thing that catches their eyeballs the most? Yes, it is images. Splendid photos put into your site not only fascinate your readers but attract them to be curious about what you have written. Sure enough, choosing the best images for your sites is serious.

Death to the Stock Photo

Death to the Stock Photo is a prestigious photo site that offers stock photos cautiously picked and provided by professional photographers all around the world. Almost every designer use these photos on their projects, in both modified and unmodified forms. This no doubt saves you tons of money without buying pro images and paying a high price.

Alternatives:

  • picjumbo
  • Unsplash
  • Moveast
